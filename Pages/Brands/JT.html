JT Sprockets

JT is a world leader when it comes to the manufacturer of aftermarket sprockets, constantly raising the industry's standards for quality and service. They are committed to supplying the most durable, best quality sprocket available in the marketplace worldwide. This is why they produce and sell more sprockets than all the other aftermarket brands combined.

 

Raw Materials

JT sprockets are made using only the best available materials. In lightweight race and competition sprockets Aviation-grade 7075-T6 aluminum alloy is used. Front sprockets are manufactured using high grade SCM415 Chromoly Steel and JT is the only sprocket manufacturer using the ultra-durable high carbon C49 steel for standard fitment rear sprockets.

 

Production

The JT sprocket factory is the largest and most advanced in the world. It is fully equipped with highly accurate machinery, including the latest generation of CNC computer design and computer controlled metal cutting equipment. Through accurate machining, JT ensures maximum durability and performance.

JT Sprockets are manufactured the right way - by hobbing, machining and drilling. This is the only proven way to achieve the closest of tolerances and the most accurate teeth profiles.

Heat treated and hand finished to perfection, all JT sprockets meet or exceed the highest possible quality standards set for the motorcycle industry.

 

Quality Control

Every sprocket goes through many production stages including 10 individual quality control checks and needs to achieve the highest European quality standards before it is ready to leave the production facility and factory.

 

Distribution

Exceptionally large stocks are always kept ready for immediate delivery, with a national network of leading agents and retailers Australia Wide, LINK International and JT Sprockets offers you a superior quality product and the service that you expect.

 

Model Availability

JT designs and produces over 18 million sprockets per year for all makes and models of motorcycles and ATVs. The current range is over 2,500 types of steel or aluminum sprockets.
